Dr. Alex Morgan

Astrophysicist & Research Scientist

Welcome to my research website. I specialize in observational cosmology and galaxy formation. My work focuses on understanding the evolution of galaxies through multi-wavelength observations and theoretical modeling.

Currently, I am a Senior Research Fellow at the International Space Science Institute, where I lead projects on dark matter distribution and cosmic structure formation.

Research

Galaxy Formation and Evolution

Investigating the physical processes that drive galaxy evolution across cosmic time, with a focus on the role of environment and feedback mechanisms.

Galaxy Clusters Star Formation Chemical Evolution

Dark Matter Distribution

Mapping dark matter in galaxy clusters using gravitational lensing techniques and studying its connection to baryonic matter.

Weak Lensing N-body Simulations Halo Models

High-Redshift Universe

Studying the earliest galaxies and quasars to understand the epoch of reionization and the formation of the first structures.

JWST ALMA Reionization

CLASH: Cluster Lensing And Supernova Survey

Multi-cycle treasury program with the Hubble Space Telescope to map the distribution of dark matter in 25 massive galaxy clusters.

Completed HST Dark Matter

Galaxy Zoo: Understanding Galaxy Morphology

Citizen science project classifying galaxy morphologies for over a million galaxies from SDSS and Hubble imaging.

Completed Citizen Science Morphology

COSMOS-Web: JWST Treasury Survey

Largest JWST Cycle 1 survey program mapping 0.6 square degrees with NIRCam and MIRI to study galaxy assembly and dark matter.

Ongoing JWST High-z Galaxies

ALMA Galaxy Cluster Survey

Studying molecular gas in galaxy clusters to understand environmental effects on star formation and galaxy evolution.

Ongoing ALMA Molecular Gas
